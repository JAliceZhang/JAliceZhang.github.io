Projects

Hand Function & Depression Among U.S. Adults

Stata

NHANES 2021–2023 · Epidemiologic study

  • Cross-sectional study (n = 1,546) examining whether difficulty using hands/fingers predicts depression, measured via PHQ-9 as both a binary and continuous outcome.
  • Built crude and multivariable logistic/linear regression models, adjusting for diabetes, smoking, and medication use, and tested general health as an effect modifier.
  • Hand difficulty remained associated with depression after adjustment: aOR 1.98 (1.48–2.65), β 1.55, p < 0.001.

Breast Cancer Diagnosis Classification

R

Machine learning course project

  • Built and compared five classifiers (logistic regression, LDA, KNN, decision tree, random forest) on 30 FNA biopsy features (n = 569).
  • 5-fold cross-validation, evaluated on AUC, sensitivity, and specificity.
  • Best model reached AUC 0.9997 with 100% sensitivity on held-out data.
