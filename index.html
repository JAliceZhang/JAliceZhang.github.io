Projects

Hand Function & Depression Among U.S. Adults

Stata

NHANES 2021–2023 · Epidemiologic study

  • Cross-sectional study (n = 1,546) examining whether difficulty using hands/fingers predicts depression, measured via PHQ-9 as both a binary and continuous outcome.
  • Built crude and multivariable logistic/linear regression models, adjusting for diabetes, smoking, and medication use, and tested general health as an effect modifier.
  • Hand difficulty remained associated with depression after adjustment: aOR 1.98 (1.48–2.65), β 1.55, p < 0.001.

Breast Cancer Diagnosis Classification

R

Machine learning course project

  • Built and compared five classifiers (logistic regression, LDA, KNN, decision tree, random forest) on 30 FNA biopsy features from the UCI WDBC dataset (n = 569, stratified 80/20 split).
  • Tuned hyperparameters via 5-fold cross-validation (KNN k=14, random forest mtry=3) and evaluated on accuracy, sensitivity, specificity, and AUC.

COVID-19 Epidemiological Trends

R

Time-series analysis · national + state-level U.S. data, 2020

  • Built daily new-case counts and 7-day moving averages from cumulative NYT case data for the U.S. and all 50 states plus territories.
  • Identified the first-wave peak date for all 55 states/territories and found peaks spread from July to January, with most states peaking in November–December, driving the national winter surge.
